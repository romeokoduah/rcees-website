UNEP E-mobility Policy Working Group

Multi-stakeholder working group convening government, industry and civil society to advance Ghana’s e-mobility policy framework, fiscal incentives and just-transition pathways under UNEP coordination.

Active 2026 – ongoing UNEP
About

Overview

The Policy Working Group is a standing convening platform — chaired with RCEES support — that aligns the public sector, private industry, financiers and civil society around a shared national e-mobility agenda. It complements the supply-side regulatory work-stream by focusing on demand-side policy, fiscal incentives, and the social and labour-market dimensions of the transition.

The Group meets on a recurring cadence to review evidence, coordinate positions, and produce policy briefs that feed directly into Ghana’s e-mobility roadmap and UNEP’s regional programme reporting.

What we’re doing

Project Scope & Objectives

  1. Convene a multi-stakeholder working group to shape Ghana’s national e-mobility policy and roadmap.
  2. Coordinate dialogue between ministries, regulators, OEMs, financiers and civil-society organisations.
  3. Produce evidence-based policy briefs on fiscal incentives, just-transition pathways and local-content rules.
  4. Track implementation progress on national e-mobility commitments and international reporting obligations.
  5. Build institutional capacity through training, peer learning and South–South knowledge exchange.
How the work is organised

Workstreams

  • Policy & fiscal design: tax incentives, duty exemptions, public procurement quotas and concessional finance instruments.
  • Just transition & labour: reskilling pathways for ICE-sector workers, MSME inclusion and gender-responsive design.
  • Local content & industry: assembly, components and after-sales-service ecosystem development.
  • Monitoring & reporting: KPIs, data infrastructure and reporting against UNFCCC/NDC commitments.
  • Capacity building: peer-learning sessions, study tours and South–South knowledge exchange with partner countries.
