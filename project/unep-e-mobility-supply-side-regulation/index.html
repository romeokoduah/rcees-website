Energy Transition

UNEP E-mobility Supply Side Regulation

Designing the supply-side regulatory architecture — technical standards, grid integration, tariffs and licensing — required to scale electric-mobility infrastructure in Ghana under UNEP’s e-mobility programme.

Active 2026 – ongoing UNEP
About

Overview

This work-stream supports Ghana’s transition to electric mobility by establishing the supply-side regulatory architecture needed for credible private investment in charging infrastructure. RCEES leads technical and policy analysis covering grid-integration standards, tariff and revenue design, vehicle homologation, battery end-of-life management, and consumer-protection rules for licensed charge-point operators.

The project is delivered under UNEP’s e-mobility programme in coordination with relevant national regulators, the utility, and ministerial counterparts. Outputs feed directly into Ghana’s e-mobility roadmap and the regional dialogue on harmonised standards across West Africa.

What we’re doing

Project Scope & Objectives

  1. Develop supply-side regulatory frameworks for EV charging infrastructure deployment in Ghana.
  2. Establish technical standards, grid-integration and interoperability requirements for e-mobility supply equipment.
  3. Assess utility readiness, tariff design and revenue mechanisms for licensed charging service providers.
  4. Review import duties, vehicle homologation and battery end-of-life rules to de-risk private-sector entry.
  5. Recommend safety, consumer-protection and licensing instruments for charge-point operators.
How the work is organised

Workstreams

  • Standards & interoperability: connector types, communication protocols, metering and safety standards aligned with IEC/ISO references.
  • Grid & utility readiness: hosting-capacity studies, demand profiles, time-of-use and demand-response tariff design.
  • Market structure & licensing: licensing categories, tariff caps, dispute resolution and consumer-protection frameworks for CPOs and e-MSPs.
  • Import, homologation & circularity: duty regimes, vehicle type-approval, and battery second-life and end-of-life obligations.
  • Stakeholder engagement: public consultations, regulator workshops and harmonisation dialogue with ECOWAS partners.
